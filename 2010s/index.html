Making Space for: "A Handmade Web"

Chia Amisola - a 2025 homage to J. R. Carpenter

Article: “Making space for a handmade web”

OBSERVATION

"There’s a resurgence of small, handcrafted sites challenging the current trajectory of the internet. Joining the movementis as simple as making your own."

TAKING ACTION

“What if we didn’t think of a website as one thing that answers to a million people? What if instead, there were millions of websites, each one handmade by a single person?”

1. The internet used to be more personal. These sites were often amateaur, messy, and expressive, but they reflected individual identity and communities.

2. Modern internet spaces have become standardized. Today's web is dominated by large platforms and social media, which encourage uniform design and algorithm-driven content.

3. People increasingly hide authentic expression online. Many users now self-censor because of public vibility and social judgement.

4. A revival of small, handmade websites is happening.

Internet Archive

Non-profit digital library offering free universal access to texts as well as 624 billion archived web pages.

DWeb

A web that connects people projects and protocols to build a decentralized web.

Rhizome

An international hub for new media art since 1996. Since 2003, Rhizome has been an affiliate in residence at the New Museum of Contemporary Art in New York City.

Zentrum für Netzkunst

An international platform connecting artists and technicians for interactive, web-based art. The initiative researches archiving and the contextualization of net art.

The Geocities Gallery

A restored visual gallery of the archived Geocities sites, sorted by neighborhood.

Neocities

Launched in 2013 by Kyle Drake, Neocities.org is a commercial web hosting service for static pages, aiming to revive the spirit of the defunct GeoCities.
