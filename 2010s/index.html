Making Space for: "A Handmade Web"

Chia Amisola - a 2025 homage to J. R. Carpenter

Article: “Making space for a handmade web”

"There’s a resurgence of small, handcrafted sites challenging the current trajectory of the internet. Joining the movement is as simple as making your own."

“What if we didn’t think of a website as one thing that answers to a million people? What if instead, there were millions of websites, each one handmade by a single person?”

1. The internet used to be more personal. These sites were often amateaur, messy, and expressive, but they reflected individual identity and communities.

2. Modern internet spaces have become standardized. Today's web is dominated by large platforms and social media, which encourage uniform design and algorithm-driven content.

3. People increasingly hide authentic expression online. Many users now self-censor because of public vibility and social judgement.

4. A revival of small, handmade websites is happening.
